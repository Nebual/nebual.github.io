> ## "Want magic? Come to Greenstone!"
>
> [there is no further elaboration]  
> -- a sign posted in Port-Town, Chult, as feeble marketing material for the school

Flint's better brother Trent, having taken the long boat ride from the Capital, is delighted (and slightly disappointed) to reunite with his brother. He reminds Flint of his wedding obligations (err uhh yes, Flint definitely remembered this promise...? brb heading to the Steam Plane). While giving him the grand tour, a very injured pair of travellers stumble into town, what looks like a learned mage and a very injured assistant. They explain that they were on their way to a magical dig site, acompanying , but were attacked in the night by some sort of glowing Tiefling dwarf thing with a pair of axes... and he wasn't alone. Sounds unfortunately like our old friend Malic Rumblepunch, having probably fallen even deeper into his hotpot of curses.

> "Err right, and as the sensitivity training says, lizardmen _are people_..."  
> -- The well learned visitor

Feeling partially responsible (due to knowing Malic), town sends out a posseee to track him down, along with any survivor's (or their loot). Arriving at their camp site:

> Given the number of litres of blood spilled, and the number of victims, one can conclude... there's nowhere near enough blood. And the very easily discernable shapes of skeletal raptors.  
> -- Trent doing a visual calculus roll, based on his medicine (dentistry)

The trail is not hard to follow. It appears the survivors(?) were dragged through the jungle, along a path that eventually connects to more paths where even larger things were dragged, seemingly towards some ritual point. Not long after, we find another ancient lizardmen ruins, and it has smoke coming from the center of it -- very bad news!

> Misora claims to be an Aasimar because she was touched inappropriately by an angel as a child. Presumably she was half-dwarf half-tiefling before.  
> New animal detected! Ken turns into a.............. Chultian Humming Bird (its like, crow sized)

Giant Humming-Ken scouts us a primo route, allowing us to avoid the skeleton-raptor-jockeys and other nonsense and enter the town ruins proper, showing a necro-priest presiding over a grand pot, with undead shuffling trays of _meat_ implements into it. The scene is eerily familiar to one we once saw back in Heck, but Heck stuff ain't supposed to be up here, even if its Chult.

> Bigger Blacker Skeletons

> "Alright, what's the range on your _dental assistants?_"
