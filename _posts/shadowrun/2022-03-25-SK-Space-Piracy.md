The hacker _Whitenoise_ contacts us, having looked into corp suicide squads like ours. A decade ago there were many rumours of corps doing these, but they were concluded to be unsubstantiated and they fizzled out, until rather recently it came up again in the dark webs. One common pattern is that eventually, the team always seems to get removed somehow, either as a cleanup or screwup. He wants to help us get out, so offers to examine one of the implants, and Styx happily volunteers.

Approaching his hacker bar meetup, he struggles to connect to Styx due to the luddite's complete lack of access ports. Bob's proved more fruitful, and confirmed there is a cranial bomb pinging a Seattle tower, but also a secondary mod powered by it...

**On the morning of the launch**, our contact explained the Shadowlands data we harvested earlier was the Mitsuhama Commercial station _Heaven_'s orbital data, but offset a bit, which they determined was actually a secret Research station hiding in the shadow of the larger station, keeping _Heaven_ between Earth and it. We'll start with a ticket to _Heaven_, meet an operative who has a ship for us, and then fly it to the Research Station. Once there, we plant a backpack (which smells suspiciously of phosphorous) along the station's spine, connect to the comms station, and then flee by our ship.


## Orbital Commercial Station: _Heaven_

Our touristy ride up went well, except for those of us who need a manasphere, who were oh so very seasick. _Heaven_'s port backs onto a Vegas-like strip, with all the eccentric & questionable amenities of an extraterritorial extravagence that caters to the ultra-wealthy.

Chester schmoozed with the VIP's, and heard that there seems to be a lot of Mitsu operatives wandering around, trying to blend in badly, may be some sort of security or running some social experiment. Awhile back someone saw a blip on a scanner up in the bridge that looked like a station out past us, but they fixed it real quick. After many drinks, one engineer admitted to being yanked onto a shuttle for an emergency repair job, drove 30m, doors opened to a different station that was much smaller and yet evidently very close to _Heaven_. They had him fix a docking port, everything there was built very redundant, everything can be fixed from inside or out.

The rest of the gang decided to relax and enjoy the amenities of the station, with some people gambling, Raptor won some money on the slots; Styx decided slots seemed like a good idea; as does Bob Ross; Hawkeye hangs around; and Nightshade has some drinks at the bar.

Styx and Chester get closer under the guise of needing directions, and manage to drunkenly get their hands on their shoulders. Meanwhile, Nightshade and Raptor continue down the hall and get stopped by three more agents, who ask (with guns drawn) to accompany them back to the centre ring.

Once the smoke cleared, we hid the bodies in the docking ring and ran aboard our new ship: it looks like a fancy, sleek, very fast ship, that's been painted to look like a run-down salvage hauler. With a guard's gunshot still ringing through the halls, we blasted off towards the Research station.

## Hidden Research Station: _Shadowed_

Hawkeye is at the helm, guiding us with stealth to the station, using the shadows cast from Heaven to hide us from anything that could be potentially looking for us. Bob Ross works with Hawkeye to hide the electronic systems from being discovered and does a damned good job at it.

We discuss our plans for how best to infiltrate the station, given the docking doors are closed, and decide to start with the comms array exterior. Bob plugs in, with the rest of us preparing to jump any engineers that come out of access hatches, but is unable to directly control it from outside, and opts instead some light sabotage. Sure enough some EVA suits come out of a hatch, and Bob's Doberman drone (which was flown over by 10x Dragonflies) lights em up easily, with Raptor and Night entering the hatch they left open.

Hacking through the last blast door to command, they easily intimidate the few nerds inside, and enable the exterior comms controls for Bob to work with. We suddenly receive a video call from our handler, who sullenly explains that SK has terminated our squad program and that our heads are going to explode the moment we restore Matrix access. He at least had the courtesy to send us the cranial bomb schematics, before we disconnect the comms array. Screw SK. Nightshade and Raptor book it to the station's medbay. Chester encourages the others to go on ahead to the medbay, he'll stay on the ship alone waiting for them - no really its fine you can trust him, go ahea---

Chester and Hawkeye see a giant railgun round blast towards the station, which gets shattered at the last moment by station defences, sending shrapnel through the station's top ring and putting giant holes in our poor ship. While it spirals out of control towards the station, Chester and Hawkeye run to the airlock, leaping just in time for the ship to decapitate the station's top ring.

## Brain Surgery

The six of us reunite in medbay, having seen many scared people running through the halls to safety, paying us no mind. Medbay has a shiny highlevel autodoc that Styx lies down in and Hawkeye begins to program the autodoc. We _phone a friend_ in to _Whitenoise_ over satelite, who adds his safe-disable mod to the Autodoc's procedure. The second mod spotted earlier turns out to be an additional explosive embedded in the heart, wired to the first, but Hawkeye's surprising _trauma surgery_ specialization combined with _improvised explosives_ familiarity allowed her to remove the payload and reinsert the empty cranial bomb. A tense series of similar surgeries clear all the brain booms, intersperced with more railgun shrapnel impacts for _extra fun medicine checks!_

Brains clear, we descend to the docking bays - first one's ship is quite salvaged, second one's been hit by shrapnel, and the third bay is empty - with the ship 200m away, covered in guards trying to get in. After sniping one, the rest of the guards start to panic, a follow up shot strikes another guard, which is when the _dragonfly swarm descends_, shredding them before carrying the ship to us.

Just as we're _hypothetically_ pondering going back to pick up parts from that excellent medbay, a final railgun round lands true, obliterating the station. We sailed our unregistered, probably-does-not-officially-exist, pirated space ship back to Earth - landing us _nowhere near Seattle_.
